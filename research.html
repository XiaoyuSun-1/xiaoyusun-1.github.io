Research

Selected Research & Projects

My work sits at the intersection of machine learning, neuroscience, biomedical imaging, and clinical software. I am especially interested in building technically strong methods that remain meaningful in real human and medical settings.

Retinal OCT Fluid Segmentation

At Washington University in St. Louis, I compared task-specific and foundation models for retinal OCT fluid detection and segmentation on the RETOUCH challenge dataset. I also developed Python pipelines for preprocessing, training, inference, and quantification.

EEG Motor-Imagery Decoding

In computational neuroscience, I adapted TIMBRE for human EEG motor-imagery decoding and benchmarked rCSP, cCSP, and TIMBRE on BCI Competition III Dataset IVa. This work explores the value of complex-valued methods for neural decoding.

Pupil Dynamics and Learning

I also analyze large-scale pupil data from the HEXXED puzzle game to study engagement, learning patterns, and performance differences across players.

Project Work

Project Femto
Harvey Mudd College Clinics

I designed a Qt touchscreen interface for femtosecond laser planning with synchronized parameter editing, interactive geometry tools, clinician feedback loops, and export workflows for surgical planning.

Computational Topology
Claremont Graduate University

I applied topological data analysis to epidemiological data, focusing on persistent homology, classification, and prediction across infection phases and locations.
